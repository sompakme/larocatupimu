7 Best Firm Mattresses For A Comfortable Sleep

If Goldilocks taught us anything, it’s that cloud nine looks different for everyone – and for some, a super soft and plush mattress isn’t that dreamy.

Firm mattresses can offer great support and take pressure off your joints, which is especially beneficial for office workers (and anyone who sits for long periods), according to Easy Sleep Solutions Sleep Consultant, Samantha Sadighi.

“It can help support your body as your bones take most of the pressure, relieving pressure on your muscles. It also takes pressure off the lower back, which can be a massive problem for many people,” she explained to news.com.au.

“Firmer mattresses tend to be more comfortable for back and front sleepers, whereas side sleepers might prefer a softer mattress.”

But how hard is too hard? Samantha suggests picturing yourself spending long periods of time in your favourite position to get an idea of whether a mattress will be comfortable enough.

“Does it feel comfortable? Lots of mattresses will naturally get softer with time (depending on the quality) so worth bearing this in mind. Also, if you share your bed, it’s worth trying to compromise on the firmness with your partner!” she said.

MATTRESS FIRMNESS SCALE

To get an idea of whether a mattress is right for you, mattresses are rated against a firmness scale to indicate how soft or hard the mattress feels. The scale is numbered from one to 10 – the higher the number, the firmer the mattress. Medium firm mattresses generally sit around 6.

But keep in mind that comfort and firmness is very subjective and varies depending on factors like body weight, so view the ratings as a general guide. People with a heavier frame typically find firmer mattresses more comfortable, while those with a lighter body type may prefer a softer feel.

It’s worth noting that the firmness scale doesn’t show how well a supportive a mattress is. Support is all about how well it relieves pressure points keeps your spine aligned, while firmness mainly refers to how the surface feels.
