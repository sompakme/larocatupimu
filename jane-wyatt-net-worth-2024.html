Early Life and Career

Jane Wyatt was born in Campgaw, New Jersey, and was raised in New York City. She attended the Chapin School and later Barnard College. Wyatt’s acting career began on Broadway in the 1930s, and she made her film debut in “One More River” in 1934. Her career spanned over six decades, with her last appearance in the television series “Star Trek” in 1996.

Major Roles and Accolades

Wyatt’s most notable role was in the television series “Father Knows Best,” where she played Margaret Anderson from 1954 to 1960. Her performance earned her three Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series. She also starred in the film “Lost Horizon,” for which she received critical acclaim.
