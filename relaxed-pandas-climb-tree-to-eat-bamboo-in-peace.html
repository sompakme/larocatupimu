Relaxed pandas climb tree to eat bamboo in peace

Scientists in China have captured intimate photos of two giant pandas eating bamboo in the canopy of a Sichuan forest.

The furry giants were snapped hanging out at the Shenshuping Base of China Conservation and Research Center for the Giant Panda in central China.

The bears are native to central China and are found nowhere else on Earth.

Pandas subsist almost entirely on bamboo, eating as much as 14kg of the plant in a single day to keep itself going.

It is rare for two pandas to be seen together, as they are primarily solitary in adult life. As they are territorial animals, it is rare to see two adult pandas tolerating each others' presence.

The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) estimates there are just over 1,800 giant pandas left in the wild, with a further 400 living in captivity.
