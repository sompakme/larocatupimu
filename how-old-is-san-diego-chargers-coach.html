Cracking the Code: How Old is the Mystery Man Leading the Los Angeles Chargers?

Ah, the age-old question that plagues football fans and pub trivia nights alike: just how old is the head coach of the Los Angeles Chargers? Buckle up, because this isn't your average "look it up online" situation. We're about to dive into a thrilling saga of team relocation, coaching changes, and maybe even a sprinkle of conspiracy theories (probably not, but hey, it's the internet!).

First, Let's Dispel a Myth: The San Diego Shuffle

For many, the Chargers will forever be synonymous with sunny San Diego beaches. But in 2017, the team relocated to Los Angeles, adding a whole new wrinkle to their history. So, when you ask about the San Diego Chargers' coach, you're technically asking about a team that doesn't physically exist anymore. Mind. Blown.

The Current Charger in Charge: Jim Harbaugh and the Age of Smarts

As of today, June 28th, 2024, the Los Angeles Chargers are led by the ever-intense and charismatic Jim Harbaugh. Fun fact: Jim Harbaugh could probably bench press most of us while simultaneously quoting Shakespeare. This coaching powerhouse isn't giving away his age easily, but a quick Google search (or a peek at his driver's license, if you happen to have that lying around) reveals he was born in 1964, making him a youthful 60 years old.

Wait, Wasn't There a Different Coach Last Season?

You'd be right! The 2023 season saw Giff Smith take the helm after the departure of Brandon Staley. But fear not, trivia aficionados! Giff's reign was temporary, and Jim Harbaugh is here to stay... for now.

So, How Old is the Chargers Coach? It Depends on Who You Ask!

Here's the takeaway:

  • The San Diego Chargers (RIP) don't have a current coach, but their last coach, Brandon Staley, is 41 years old (as of June 2024).
  • The Los Angeles Chargers are led by the fiery Jim Harbaugh, a sprightly 60-year-old.

Bonus Round: How Old Were Some Legendary Charger Coaches?

  • Don Coryell, the offensive mastermind behind "Air Coryell": 85 years old at the time of his passing (legend never dies, baby!)
  • Marty Schottenheimer, the man who almost brought a Lombardi Trophy to San Diego: 77 years old
