Remembering Bishop Carlton Pearson: A Brief Overview of His Life and Exploring the Circumstances of

News: Bishop Carlton Pearson, a visionary minister and former megachurch founder, has passed away at the age of 70 after bravely battling cancer. Although he is no longer with us in the flesh, his enduring legacy of challenging conventional beliefs and championing love and equality will serve as an ongoing source of inspiration for future generations.

Early Life and Ministry

On March 19, 1953, Bishop Carlton D’Metrius Pearson emerged as not just an American minister but also a notable gospel music artist. In the 1990s, he took the helm of the Higher Dimensions Evangelistic Center in Tulsa, drawing a consistent congregation of more than 6,000 worshipers. Pearson’s dynamic preaching style and undeniable charisma propelled him to a rising star status within the Pentecostal preaching circuit.

A Shift in Theology

In 2008, Bishop Pearson captured widespread attention when he embraced what he termed the “Gospel of Inclusion.” Taking a bold stance, he rejected the concept of hell and championed a more inclusive interpretation of God’s love. This theological departure triggered significant criticism and ultimately resulted in the closure of his church. Despite facing intense backlash, Pearson stood firm in his dedication to these transformative beliefs.

Championing Love and Equality

As early as 2004, well before his significant shift in theology, Bishop Pearson encountered formidable opposition. Some members of his religious community branded him a heretic due to his advocacy for gay rights. Undeterred, Pearson remained steadfast in his commitment, persistently championing principles of love, acceptance, and equality for all individuals.

A Compassionate Life Coach

In addition to his role as a minister, Pearson left a lasting impact as a compassionate life coach, guiding individuals toward personal growth and understanding. His remarkable ability to empathize and connect with people on a profound level left an indelible mark on the lives he touched.
