The 16 Best Bikini Trimmers of 2024

Summer is near, which means you’re probably thinking about spending lots of time in the sun. If you’re looking to escape the cold and head to the beach, pool, or anywhere else you might be donning a swimsuit, dealing with your bikini line is undoubtedly on your mind. There aren’t many pleasant options for managing unwanted hair in the area — using a waxing kit can be painful and shaving can trigger razor bumps and sensitivity. Although there are alternatives, such as laser treatments or the (somewhat) less painful sugar wax, they can feel a little daunting for pain-averse people. A great alternative? At-home bikini trimmers.

Bikini trimmers are electric hair removal devices that cut hair without blades touching the skin, minimizing the risk of skin irritations and ingrown hairs. Because they don’t directly glide over the skin, they tend to be a “safer option for those with sensitive skin,” says NYC-based board-certified dermatologist Dr. Marisa Garshick. And best of all, it can be done in less than 10 minutes from the comfort of your own home. Bikini trimmers are also a gift from heaven for anyone with sensitive skin or is prone to ingrown hairs, as trimmers don't actually ever touch the skin; they just hover.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Trimmer vs. Razor: Still not sure which to opt for? In order to choose, let’s first define the differences between the two. According to Dr. Garshick, “Bikini trimmers use floating blades that help to shorten the hairs, unlike razors which shave the hairs and may pull at the skin”. Trimmers are less likely to cause ingrown hairs, she explains, so anyone prone to this skin condition may prefer a bikini trimmer. If you have an adjustable trimmer, you can choose the length of hair that the trimmer will snip.

    As for the number of blades, board-certified dermatologist Dr. Lauren Penzi recommends “at least two for the majority of people, and three if you are prone to irritation and/or ingrown hairs.” The higher the number of blades, “the more efficient the trimming process,” she explains. 

  • Trim Lengths: If you desire more creative control over how short you want the hair to be cut, then we recommend opting for a trimmer with multiple trim lengths. These options come with guards that can be adjusted and block the space between the skin and the blade at your desired length. This way, you can change the look of your underarm hair versus your bikini line and get more usage out of your bikini trimmer.
  • Features: Many of today’s top bikini trimmers offer a range of helpful features. Some, but not all, are waterproof, allowing you to trim while in the shower. It’s important to pay attention to this distinction before you make a purchase as you don’t want to accidentally carry a non-waterproof electric device into the water stream. Some models also come with safety attachments, various comb heads for different trimming results, and LED lights for improved visibility.
  • Water Resistance: The nice difference between a razor and a trimmer is that you can go water-free with a trimmer. That being said, if you prefer to accomplish everything in one go under the shower, there are plenty of water-resistant options on this list that allow you to take your trimmer into the shower. Do not accidentally take your non-water-resistant trimmer into the shower as it can be a hazard.
  • Skin Type: According to both Dr. Penzi and board-certified dermatologist Dr. Divya Shokeen, bikini trimmers are safe for all skin types, including sensitive skin, eczema, and keratosis pilaris. “Trimmers cut the hair straight across, unlike shaving, and usually have built-in safety features like rounded blades or guards to prevent nicks and irritation,” says Dr. Penzi. To boot, Dr. Shokeen adds that “they are easier on those with sensitive skin because no products or irritants are necessarily applied in order to use a trimmer.”

Your Questions, Answered

Are bikini trimmers safe to use?

Overall, bikini trimmers are safe to use, but according to Dr. Garshick, if they’re not used correctly, they can contribute to cuts, ingrown hairs, scarring, and skin irritation.

Trimmers are less likely to cause ingrown hairs compared to using razors and shaving cream, but it’s still a possibility. “Given the skin in the groin area is particularly delicate and sensitive, any injury to the hair can lead to potential hyperpigmentation or scarring,” says Dr. Garshick. “That said, trimmers are considered a good option for those with sensitive skin.”

How should you use a bikini trimmer?

Before you get started, check whether the trimmer is water-resistant to determine whether it can be used in the shower or not. If it is not waterproof, you’ll need to use it outside of the shower, although board-certified dermatologist Dr. Mamina Turegano says it’s best to trim the bikini line right after sudsing up to make sure the area is clean and the hairs are softened, which makes them easier to shave down. If you’re prone to ingrown hairs, swap your cleanser for a salicylic- or glycolic acid-based exfoliant to remove any dead skin cells and gunk from the area.

If it is waterproof, you’re free to use it in the shower, although you should still follow the same cleansing steps outlined above before trimming.

The best way to use a bikini trimmer, according to Dr. Garshick, is to hold the skin taut with one hand while trimming with the other to allow for a more consistent and comfortable shave. Also, make sure you shave in the direction of the hair to prevent razor burn or ingrown hairs. Lastly, make sure to change the razor every two to three weeks.

How should you treat your skin after using a bikini trimmer?

“It's important to moisturize your skin immediately after using a bikini trimmer,” says board-certified dermatologist Dr. Lindsey Zubritsky. “This helps to protect the skin barrier, reduce the risk of razor burn, and decrease the risk of irritation.” She recommends using a fragrance-free lotion or cream that is labeled as non-comedogenic, which will moisturize skin without clogging pores.

Does trimming pubic hair make it prickly?

Bikini trimmers typically do leave some stubble since they do not shave as close to the skin as traditional razors do, says Dr. Zubritsky. Trimmers don't remove the hair entirely — they just cut the hair down to a shorter length. If you're looking for a completely smooth option, consider using an epilator, waxing, or trying laser hair removal for the best results.

Should you trim your bikini line wet or dry?

“This absolutely depends on the type of trimmer you use,” says Dr. Shokeen. “Some are designed for wet skin and others may be designed for dry skin with an LED to support visualization of hair. It is always important to read the instructions for the razor you choose and go with the grain never against it!”

How do you prep skin before using a bikini trimmer?

Start by cleaning and drying (if the trimmer requires a dry shave) the areas to remove any oils or lotions,” recommends Dr. Shokeen. For those prone to infection or irritation, “it can be helpful to use an antibacterial cleanser, such as one containing benzoyl peroxide, chlorhexidine, or povidone iodine,” chimes in Dr. Garshick.
