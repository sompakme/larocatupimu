WWE WrestleMania 37 Night 1 Results For April 10, 2021

WWE WrestleMania 37 Night 1 will go down tonight and Ringside News has got you covered with our exclusive live play-by-play results coverage.

Start time for WrestleMania is 7:00 PM EST, with the Kickoff-show starting at 6:00 PM EST. Match-by-match highlights and results from the show will take place on this very page. Feel free to leave any reactions in the comment section below.

Tonight is the first half of the biggest wrestling PPV of the year. WrestleMania is always an exciting show but this year’s is extra special because we will finally have a sizeable audience in attendance for the first time in over a year. And starting tonight’s show is the man everyone thought deserved his big moment in front of a crowd for carrying RAW for the past year, Drew McIntyre. Tonight McIntyre goes one-on-one with Bobby Lashley in an attempt to win back his WWE Championship.

Of course, the main event tonight will see Royal Rumble winner Bianca Belair take on SmackDown Women’s Champion, Sasha Banks. While this match has had a fairly basic build-up, online voices demanded the ladies be the main event and so here we are. Regardless of storyline, this match should deliver between the ropes but it will have a lot to do in order to outshine the others on the card.

Speaking of which, the rest of Night 1 consists of a steel cage match between Shane McMahon and Braun Strowman, Cesaro Vs. Seth Rollins in a singles match, The Miz & John Morrison Vs. Damian Priest and Bad Bunny, the RAW Tag Team Title match between The New Day and AJ Styles and Omos, and a Tag Team Turmoil match to determine the number one contenders for the Women’s Tag Team Titles tomorrow night.

And that’s the rundown for WrestleMania Night 1. We want to know which match you’re most looking forward to, as well as your hype levels heading into this massive event. So sound off below, hit that refresh button to follow along live, and enjoy the show!

Kick-Off Show

The WrestleMania Kick-Off Show is hosted by Kayla Braxton and she’s joined by Booker T, Jerry Lawler, JBL, and Peter Rosenberg. The fans in attendance chant for JBL and he encourages it. The panel joke around some before running down the card for tonight’s WrestleMania, which starts with Drew McIntyre Vs. Bobby Lashley for the WWE Championship.

We get a video package for Bianca Belair Vs. Sasha Banks for the SmackDown Women’s Championship. They point out that Belair and Banks will become the first two women to main event a WrestleMania in a singles match. JBL says, just like Ron Simmons when he won the title, this has nothing to do with race or gender, these women have earned it. Rosenberg says there is immense pressure on both women for different reasons, and they say this match will prove who is the best of the best.

The panel discuss The New Day Vs. AJ Styles & Omos and Booker jokingly says New Day are not great, they’re just lucky. Lawler say’s he’s excited to see Omos’ debut and thinks he’ll live up to the hype. Kayla says the bigger they are the harder they fall and The King corrects her, “the harder they hit”. Booker likens Omos to Francis Ngannou in UFC and how he’s a giant who didn’t have all the ability but still rose to the top. JBL says Ngannou didn’t have to do it as part of a team.

A video package airs of the feud between Bad Bunny, Damian Priest, The Miz, and John Morrison. Booker says Bunny took the guitar shot to the back and he got back up, which says a lot about him. Rosenberg says Bunny is a monster in pop music and he’s been putting in the work to prepare for this match. JBL and Lawler say tonight is the night the music dies when Miz and Morrison teach Bunny a lesson. Booker says Bunny has been in fights and he loves WWE but JBL says he loves football but he can’t score a touchdown. The panel is split on this one.

Highlights are shown of the two Hall of Fame ceremonies from earlier this week. JBL shows-off his new ring after a year-long wait. JBL says it means so much to him as a kid who was a Hulkamaniac, and to go in the same night as Hogan did with the NWO was special.

The panel run down tomorrow night’s card, then talk about which match they’re most excited about tonight. The next match they talk about is Braun Strowman Vs. Shane McMahon inside a steel cage. They talk about whether or not Shane has bitten off more than he can chew. Lawler echoes Shane and calls Braun stupid, then JBL says he’ll never bet against a McMahon.

Sonya Deville joins the panel to discuss the Tag Team Turmoil match. Kayla asks her how she feels about the team of Mandy Rose and Dana Brooke and Sonya says she and Rose were obviously better because she was in the team. Deville says she will have her eyes on this match because of the huge ramifications it might have on the women’s division.

The panel run down the card one last time, then discuss the opening match, Drew McIntyre VS. Bobby Lashley. They quickly make their predictions for the match with JBL and Booker T siding with Lashley, and Lawler saying McIntyre will rise to the occasion. That’s it for the Kick-Off Show. WrestleMania Night 1 is starting right now, all you have to do is hit the refresh button below and sound-off in the comments!

WrestleMania

Night 1 of WrestleMania 37 gets underway in Raymond James Satdium with 25,000 and Vince McMahon standing on the stage with most of the roster. Vince says this past year the WWE Superstars performed tirelessly for the fans but there was something missing. The most important thing of all: the fans. After a terrible year, on behalf of the entire WWE family, Vince welcomes everyone to WrestleMania. Bebe Rhexa attempts ‘America The Beautiful’.

As soon as the introduction video package airs, Michael Cole informs us that we’re suffering another weather delay and we’ll have to wait a little while. Earlier today there was a weather delay that saw the fans evacuate the stadium and some of the set get damaged. Hopefully this one doesn’t last too long but we already know the first match of the night and it’s a big one!

Sarah Schrieber is backstage with Shane McMahon and she asks how it feels to have the fans back. Shane says he had goosebumps and having the fans is the energy boost he needs to take-out Braun Strowman. Sarah says Shane has been bullying Braun and Shane says he hasn’t. He says he’s been using his brain, unlike Braun, and he has a few surprises in store for the cage match.

Bobby Lashley and MVP walk-up and interrupt Shane’s interview. MVP says the time for talking is done. This moment is to be the crowning of the All Mighty era. He says the only thing Drew McIntyre understands is violence and that’s the only language Lashley speaks. Drew wants the WWE Championship back but that isn’t happening. McIntyre appears and tells MVP to keep talking but MVP does his best to keep them apart. Drew says he’ll fight Lashley backstage if he has to, Mother Nature be damned.

Officials come to take Lashley away. Drew is asked where his head is at and he says he’s trying real hard to keep his emotions in check. A year ago he had his huge moment and he is so proud of everything he did but he’s got back to WrestleMania and a chance to win his title back but now the weather is trying to stop it. He doesn’t care what happens, he’s going to win that title regardless of where or how and it happens tonight.

New Day are interviewed backstage and Woods says they’ve been anticipating the return of fans for over a year but it feels so much better than they could ever have imagined. They’re asked why they agreed to fight Styles and Omos and Kofi says they love to take on all challengers and that’s why they’re the best. Woods says they’ve been fighting big guys since they both started wrestling and they can do it. They’ve beaten every team in the company. Big E randomly appears and Woods talks about how great he is and that he will retain tomorrow night. Big E says we’re gonna see why New Day are one of the greatest tag teams on Earth and we’re blessed to witness it.

Braun Strowman is interviewed backstage and he says he’s anxious to get out there and shut Shane McMahon up. He’s fighting for everyone who’s ever been bullied or made to feel stupid. Shane has backed him into a corner and he’s going to beat Shane to within an inch of his life.

Kevin Owens takes the microphone for himself backstage and recounts his history with Sami Zayn, going all the way back to 2002 and their matches in front of half a dozen in Quebec. They’ve been down this road a hundred times over twenty years, but he can guarantee no two men have ever been held down and told they’ll never make it as much as them. And now they get to do it once more on the WrestleMania stage. Sami Zayn is not the man he knew back then and tomorrow night he will Stun him, and if Logan Paul wants it he’ll be waiting.

The EST. of WWE, Bianca Belair, is interviewed. Belair says she is not scared or nervous, she is prepared and ready for this. She says she lvoes and craves competition, so she can’t wait to get in there with Sasha Banks, then walk out as the Women’s Champion. But the best part of all is having the fans back and it’s amazing.

Next up for an interview is Seth Rollins. He laughs manically and says he loves the chaos of all this. Kevin Patrick asks him about Cesaro and Seth gets annoyed. Rollins says they can talk about anything but he had to bring up Cesaro and The Swing. Week after week Cesaro has been disrespecting him and he can only assume he’s trying to use his star power to springboard into the stratosphere. Rollins says the rain will eventaully leave, the stars will shine, and he will take care of Cesaro and put on one hell of a performance.

The weather warning has passed and fans return to the arena, so out comes the hosts of WrestleMania, Titus O’Neil and Hulk Hogan. Titus welcomes us to his city and says the WWE Universe has the energy we’ve been craving, weather be damned. Hogan says we’re going to make history tonight and tomorrow night, and we’re going to get started right now with the first match of the night.

WWE Championship

(C) Bobby Lashley W/MVP Vs. Drew McIntyre

The match gets underway after ring introductions, and they lock-up and struggle for control. Lashley takes the back but Drew grabs the wrist. McIntyre gets shot off and Bobby floors him with a big shoulder tackle. McIntyre takes another headlock and again gets shot off but this time he avoids Lashley and hits a belly-to-belly and flips him off. Lashley shoves Drew in the face and they start trading back and forth until McIntyre forces him into the corner.

Lashley forearms Drew in the face but McIntyre clotheslines him over the top rope to the floor. Drew goes out but Lashley slams him into the barricade, then throws him into it a few times. Back inside the ring, Lashley hits a big shoulder tackle in the corner, followed by a neckbreaker and some ground-and-pound. Lashley clobbers the Scot with forearms, then chokes him on the bottom rope. Bobby looks for another big shoulder tackle but Drew avoids it and he hits the post. McIntyre targets the arm and looks for an armbar but Lashley punches himself free.

Lashley slaps McIntyre across the face, then backs him into the corner and shoulders him repeatedly. Drew is whipped to the opposite corner but he bounces back with a lariat. McIntyre ducks a clothesline and hits a belly-to-belly, then another. A neckbreaker and kip-up from McIntyre, he looks for the Future Shock but Lashley runs him into the buckles. Lashley eats an elbow and Drew follows-up with a northern lights suplex for two. Bobby lands a lariat and a roll-out dominator for a near-fall.

Drew hits a headbutt but the Champion retaliates with a spinebuster for two. Lashley attempts The Hurt Lock but Drew fights it and beats Lashley into the corner for a reverse Alabama slam for a near-fall. Drew puts Lashley on the top rope and chops him, then climbs up but gets dropped into the tree of woe. McIntyre sits up and throws Lashley across the ring, then looks for the Claymore but Lashley hits another spinebuster. Lashley signals for The Hurt Lock but McIntyre kips-up and Lashley can’t believe it.

They trade hands but Drew catches him with a belly-to-belly, then three Future Shock DDT’s! And Lashley kicks out. “This is awesome” from a real WWE crowd. McIntyre goes to the corner and counts down for the Claymore but Lashley rolls from the ring with MVP… only for Drew to leap over the ropes to wipe them both out! Drew puts Lashley back inside and gets on the top rope for a clothesline but he gets caught and thrown across the ring. Lashley hits a reverse STO, then looks for The Hurt Lock but Drew runs him into the buckles.

Drew runs and Lashley attempts a spinebuster but McIntyre counters with a Kimura! Lashley thinks about tapping but gets to the bottom rope. Both men get up and trade right hands in a slugfest. Lashley runs into a big boot and Drew hits a Glasgow Kiss. Drew wants a Claymore but MVP distracts him and Lashley ducks it. Lashley drills Drew with a forearm in the corner, then applies The Hurt Lock. McIntyre falls to a knee but climbs up and kicks off the turnbuckles and rolls backwards but Lashley holds on. Drew passes out.

Winner: Bobby Lashley

The fans inside Raymond James Stadium seemed genuinely shocked at that result but that was a really good match regardless.

The NWO are backstage with Titus O’Neil when Bayley appears. Bayley tells them about her famous talk show and that she can have them on and it would be too sweet. Nobody will give Bayley the too sweet gesture and walk off on her.

Our next match is women’s Tag Team Turmoil. Two teams start the match, then more teams enter after a pinfall or submission until there is one team left and they get a title shot against Shayna Baszler and Nia Jax tomorrow night.

Tag Team Turmoil Match

Naomi & Lana Vs. Carmella & Billie Kay

Naomi and Carmella start the match and Naomi clobbers Carmella with a flurry of kicks and a dropkick. Carmella is backed into the corner and Lana tags in for a bulldog. Naomi tags back in and she slams Billie into the buckles, then hits a springoard enziguiri.

Naomi knocks Carmella from the apron as Lana hits a kick, then they hit a double-team X-Factor for a near-fall. Naomi tosses Carmella from the ring and Kay rolls-up Naomi with help from Carmella for the pinfall and the first team has been eliminated.

Naomi & Lana Eliminated

Carmella takes control of Morgan and hits a bronco buster in the corner, then slams her for a two-count but Ruby rushes in to break the pin attempt. Carmella tags out and brawls with Ruby on the floor. Kay rolls-up Morgan in the ring and Carmella tries to help again but this time the referee sees it. Liv hits Kay with a gutbuster, then Ruby hits a senton for the pinfall.

Carmella & Billie Kay Eliminated

Mandy Rose & Dana Brooke Enter

After their pinfall, Carmella superkicks Ruby and Liv before leaving. Rose and Brooke come out and Mandy slipped on the wet ramp. Ruby takes the fight to Mandy and Dana before the even get in the ring but Rose kicks Ruby back. Brooke enters the match with a blockbuster from te topre but Liv breaks the pin attempt. Brooke gets frustrated and punches Riott on the mat.

Ruby fires back with right hands but gets caught with a spinning neckbreaker for two. Rose tags in and stomps Ruby in the corner but Riott fights free and rolls under a lariat to tag Morgan. Dana Brooke & Mandy Rose Eliminated

Natalya & Tamina Enter

Natalya comes in and immediately looks for the Sharpshooter on Liv but gets rolled-up for two. Natalya lifts and drops Liv on the top rope before tagging Tamina. They hit a double-team powerbomb and Ruby breaks the pin attempt. Tamina clears Ruby from the ring and clobbers Liv in the corner with knees. Liv gets put on the top rope and kicks Tamina back. Ruby tags in for a double-team wheelbarrow spear, then a superkick. Liv hits the gutbuster again and Ruby hits the top rope senton for a near-fall. Tamina knocks Liv out of the ring and tags Natalya so they hit a Hart Attack to Ruby. Natalya considers the Sahrpshooter but instead tags Tamina for a top rope splash and they score the win.

Seth Rollins Vs. Cesaro

The bell rings and Cesaro sprints across the ring to hit a European uppercut. Cesaro looks for The Swing but Rollins kicks him away. Seth escapes to the apron and slingshots over Cesaro but gets caught with a springboard European uppercut. Rollins rolls to the apron again and this time he snaps Cesaro’s arm across the rope, then heads up top.

Cesaro dropkicks Rollins and climbs up to hit a gutwrench superplex but Rollns slides down and hits a Buckle Bomb for a two-count. Seth slows things down and targets the arm. He kicks Cesaro but the Swiss Superman fires back with a right hand and whips Seth to the corner. Cesaro goes up top but Rollins leaps up alongside him and hits a superplex into the falcon arrow for a near-fall. Rollins is getting frustrated.

Cesaro catches Seth with a backslide for a two-count, then lands a series of European uppercuts. Cesaro whips Seth to the corner and hits a running uppercut, then doe sit again before catching him with a discus lariat for a near-fall. Cesaro signals for The Swing but Seth rolls him up for two. Rollins hits a rope-assisted enziguiri and looks for The Stomp but Cesaro counters with The Swing! Cesaro swings Seth for nine rotations but his arm is hurting so he stops and moves to the Sharpshooter. Rollins reaches the ropes to cause the break.

Cesaro looks for the Neutralizer but Seth flips him over and hits a slingblade. Rollins goes to the apron and hits a springboard knee, then slams Cesaro for a near-fall. Rollins goes to the top rope and hits a corkscrew frog splash for a near-fall. Rollins wants the Ripcord Knee but Cesaro avoids it and lands the Neutralizer for a near-fall! Cesaro looks like he’s starting to doubt himself. Cesaro counters Rollins into an incredible Neutralizer attempt but Seth reverses into a Pedigree but Cesaro kicks out!

Rollins talks trash to Cesaro, telling him he survived The Swing and the Neutralizer. Rollins forearms the back of his head, then hits a running kick to the back of the head. Rollins lines-up The Stomp but Cesaro catches him with an uppercut. Cesaro rallies and hits Seth with the UFO – a spinning torture rack with no hands – before letting Rollins fly across the ring. Cesaro performs The Swing for a record-breaking 23 rotations, then hits the Neutralizer for the win!

Winner: Cesaro

As soon as the match ends, Cesaro holds his head in his hands in emotion. He’s won his first-ever WrestleMania singles match and the biggest match of his career.

Omos is not exactly dressed to wrestle but he’s starting the match against Kofi Kingston. Kofi and Woods goad AJ into starting the match instead. AJ gets thrown back in the ring and Kingston hits a crossbody. Woods tags in and AJ counters a back suplex but misses an enziguiri and Kingston hits a huge springboard splash to the back for a two-count. New Day look for a double-team move but AJ knocks Kofi from the ring and Woods is stuck on the top rope, so Styles tags Omos and the stadium pops! Omos comes in to the middle of the ring and Woods kicks him to no effect. Omos lifts Kofi and hits a massive spinebuster, then stands with one boot on him for the pin!

Shane tries climbing the but Braun grabs his feet. Shane kicks him away and rips a piece of metal off the top of the cage and hits Braun with it. Shane continues to use the metal to beat Braun, then tries to crawl out but Strowman grabs his legs and drags him back and throws him up into the air.

Braun rolls to the corner and Shane goes to the top rope to hit Coast-To-Coast for a near-fall!

Banks gets on the apron but Banks runs her into ring post. Banks then dives through the ropes with a suicide dive but Belair catches her, lifts her into a military press, then carries Banks up the steps and throws her back into the ring! StrongEST indeed. 

Banks is angry and she hits a dropkick for a two-count. Belair lifts Sasha and does some squats before a body slam. Belair attempts a handspring moonsault but Banks counters by grabbing her hair and pulls it, then kicking her in the face a few times. Banks pummels Bianca on the mat with right hands, then chokes her in the corner with a boot. Banks runs her face-first into the buckles, then hits a snapmare and a chinlock. 

Belair fights up and throws Banks to the apron but Sasha clocks her with a knee. Banks looks for double knees but gets caught and slammed hard. Banks rolls outside with a handful of Belair’s braid and pulls her against the ring post, but Bianca yanks her into the steel steps! Banks stumbles back but tries to follow-up with a meteora against the barricade but Belair moves and she crashes. Both women just about make it back into the ring before the referee’s ten count.

Back inside the ring Banks looks for an inside cradle but Belair lifts her up into the air for a stalling suplex, during which she bounced Sasha off the ropes a few times and did some squats. Both women are down and slowly get to their feet. Banks drops Belair with a right hand, then grabs her by the braid and attempts a springboard arm-drag but Belair forearms her. Belair drops Sasha, then hits a standing shooting star press.  

Belair shoulders the Champion in the corner, then hits a release Glam-Slam. Belair goes to the top rope and leaps for a 450 splash but Banks got her knees up! Both women attempt pinfalls, then Sasha gets caught and powerbombed twice for a two-count. Bianca attempted a third powerbomb but Banks countered with a facebuster. Banks stomps Belair in the corner and hits a springboard tornado DDT for a near-fall.

Banks goes to the top rope and hits a five-star frog splash for another near-fall. Belair rolls to the apron but Banks slams her down by the arm, then drags her outside and tosses her into the steel steps. Banks puts Belair inside and applies the Bank Statement using the braid! Belair tries to get the ropes but Sasha rolls backwards, only for Bianca to immediately roll forward and grab the ropes. 

Banks gets angry and holds onto the move until the count of four, then kicks Bianca in the face a few times. Banks lifts Belair and tells her she’ll never be the EST but that just made her angry. Belair shoulders Banks in the corner, then lifts her onto the top rope. Sasha gets Belair in the tree of woe and stomps her a few times, then looks for a double stomp but Bianca dodges it. Belair attempts a meteora but nobody’s home and she hits the buckles. Belair hits the 450 splash but Banks kicks out! 

Belair gets mad and lifts Banks up for the K.O.D but Sasha lands on her feet and grabs the hair again. Sasha runs but Belair smashes her across the ribs with the hair. Belair lifts her for the K.O.D again but Banks slides off and attempts the Bank Statement, only for Belair to turn her around and get her up for the Kiss of Death and the pinfall! 

Winner and New SmackDown Women’s Champion: Bianca Belair
