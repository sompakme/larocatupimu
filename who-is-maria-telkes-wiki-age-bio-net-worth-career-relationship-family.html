Maria Telkes – A Pioneering Biophysicist

  • Maria Telkes, often referred to as “The Sun Queen,” was born on December 12, 1900, in Budapest, Hungary.
  • She passed away on December 2, 1995, at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of groundbreaking work in solar thermal storage systems.

Education and Innovations

  • Maria Telkes attended elementary high school and later graduated from Eotvos Lorand University with a B.A. in physical chemistry.
  • She is recognized as one of the pioneers of solar thermal storage systems, working to create versions suitable for villagers in impoverished and arid regions.
  • One of her most significant inventions was the solar distiller and the first solar-powered heating system designed for homes.
