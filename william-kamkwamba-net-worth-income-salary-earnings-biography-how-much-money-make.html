William Kamkwamba is a Malawian inventor and author who was born on August 5, 198In 2002, he made headlines in his own country when he used blue gum trees, bicycle parts, and materials found in a nearby scrapyard to build a windmill to power a few electrical appliances in his family’s Masitala home. Since then, he’s constructed a solar-powered water pump that provides the village’s first source of drinking water, as well as two additional windmills (the tallest of which stands at 39 feet) and is building two more, one of which will be in Lilongwe, Malawi’s political capital.
