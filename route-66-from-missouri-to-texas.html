Route 66: from Missouri to Texas

Distance: approx 365 miles
Drive time: 5.5 hours (non-stop) 

Ask anyone over the age of 45 about their bucket list and driving Route 66 will likely be in the top three. Named the “Mother Road” by John Steinbeck, the original route crossed eight states and three time zones and though most of the original road was decommissioned by 1984, there are still sections which make popular road trips. The 400-mile stretch between St Louis, Missouri and Amarillo, Texas starts from downtown St Louis, winds through Joplin, Cuba and Oklahoma and is dotted with historic motels, diners and kitsch roadside attractions like the 80ft-long Blue Whale of Catoosa. Make sure to take a comfort break at Pops 66 Soda Ranch in Arcadia, Oklahoma, a gas station and diner which sells 700 flavours of soda. 

Take a detour

An hour south of Oklahoma City, head towards Davis and visit Turner Falls in the Arbuckle Mountains and splash around in the state’s largest waterfalls. The Meramec Caverns in Sullivan, a five-mile system of caves deep in the Ozarks, were reputedly the hideout of Jesse James in the 1870s. 

Finally to Amarillo; make your first stop Braceros for authentic Mexican food served in an old Route 66 garage. The Jack Sisemore Traveland RV Museum is a must-see for petrol heads who’ll purr over vintage RVs from the 1930s to 1970s. Head 10 miles south-west of Downtown for the Cadillac Ranch, an art installation featuring 10 graffitied Cadillacs buried nose deep in the ground. It’s also the gateway to Palo Duro Canyon State Park, the second largest canyon in the US and a popular spot for horse riding, mountain biking and hiking. 

On the road

The USA is a land of contrasts, one best explored on two or four wheels.
