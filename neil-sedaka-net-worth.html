Neil Sedaka is a renowned pop singer and composer born in Brooklyn, NY on March 13, 1939. He is best known for his hit song "Breaking Up Is Hard to Do" released in 1962, and has since had nine #1 hits such as "Bad Blood" and "Laughter in the Rain".

About

Pop singer and Composer of "Breaking Up Is Hard to Do" in 1962. He has had nine #1 hits like "Bad Blood" and "Laughter in the Rain."

Before Fame

His mother worked a part time job to pay for the family's first piano. He attended the Juilliard School of Music's Preparatory Division for Children.

Trivia

His career has spanned more than fifty years and he's written with lyricists Howard Greenfield and Phil Cody.

Family Life

He married Leba Strassberg on September 11, 1962 and they had two children together.

Associated With

His hit "Ring-a-Rockin'" earned him the first of many appearances on Dick Clark's American Bandstand.
